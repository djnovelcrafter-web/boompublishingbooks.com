The Story

Some truths were buried to keep someone alive

When a packet of forged Victorian documents is unearthed beneath a widow’s winter rose, rare-book authenticator Beatrice Kwan must decide what every keeper of secrets eventually faces: whether a truth buried to save a life was ever meant to be dug up.

A century earlier, a governess named Bea Grey helped fake a child’s death to shield him from a predatory guardian — and was drawn into a clandestine network of women who used forged paper, false names, and buried testimony to survive a law built against them.

As the scandal threatens to surface in both centuries, Beatrice learns that the women who came before her did not simply endure. They acted. They forged. They chose. And some truths, once unearthed, can repeat the very harm they were buried to prevent.

Book One was about refusing to be written. Book Two is about what it costs to write someone else’s story — and live with the ink.
