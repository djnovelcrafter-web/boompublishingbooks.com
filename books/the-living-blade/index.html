The Story

To love the Blade is to unmake it

Valthyr will fall by dawn. With her city besieged and her people dying, High Priestess Liriya does the one thing the old laws forbid. She bleeds on the bone altar and summons the Living Blade, a god forged for war in the shape of a man. Elandor is everything the histories promised. Beautiful. Unstoppable. Empty. He does not ask who she is, or what he has lost. He asks only for a target.

He was made to end wars. She was made to lead them. Neither was made for the thing that begins to grow between a wielder and her weapon.

Because the Blade carries one flaw, burned into him at the forge that took his name: feeling undoes him. Every spark of warmth dims the light that could save Valthyr. Every step closer to the woman who summoned him drags him one step nearer to mortal, and mortal things bleed. And across the siege lines, a conqueror who believes love is the only weakness worth exploiting has already begun to guess the truth.

She summoned a god to save her city. She never imagined she would have to choose between the man she is learning to love and every soul behind her walls, or that she has worn his grief before, in a life that ended in fire.

The Cast

A wielder, a weapon, and the war between them

👑

Liriya

The Wielder

High Priestess and commander of Valthyr, who carved forbidden prayers into her own skin to summon a god. Her compassion is the strength that holds her city together, and the crack her enemy means to break.

⚔

Elandor

The Living Blade

A weapon forged for war in the shape of a man, summoned from the cosmic forge that burned away the mortal he once was. He begins as cold purpose and starlight, and the warmth that wakes in him is the very thing that is killing him.

🔮

General Varak

The Conqueror

The serpent who has swallowed seventeen cities. He does not besiege walls so much as dissect the people behind them, and he has built his power on the belief that emotion is the only weakness worth exploiting.

🔥

Morwyn

The Memory

A woman loved and lost in another lifetime, in a sun-drenched kingdom that ended on a pyre. Her face is one Liriya already wears, and her grief is the wound the Blade has carried across a thousand years.

What It's About

Some loves cost everything

Love as the Flaw

In a weapon built to feel nothing, every flicker of feeling is failure. The story asks whether love is a weakness, or the only thing worth being destroyed for.

The Cost of Command

A leader who must spend lives to save a city, and learns that the heart which makes her worth following is the lever her enemy will pull.

Personhood vs. Instrument

What turns a weapon into a person, and a person into a weapon. The answer cuts both ways, and the ending pays it back in full.

Love Across Lifetimes

A grief carried over centuries, a face worn twice, a fire that has burned before. The same souls, finding each other only to lose each other again.

For readers of The Poppy War (R.F. Kuang), These Violent Delights (Chloe Gong), This Is How You Lose the Time War (El-Mohtar & Gladstone), and the doomed-fated-mates ache of A Court of Thorns and Roses — a love story with teeth and an ending that earns its tears.

The Book

A complete standalone

One siege. One summoning. One love, across two lifetimes, with one ending. The Living Blade is a self-contained tragedy in thirty-one chapters, written to break your heart on purpose.

Adult dark fantasy. Contains battlefield violence, two open-door love scenes, and a tragic ending that does not flinch.

The Last Prayer

The siege engines burned like funeral pyres against the dying light, and Liriya knew that Valthyr would fall before the next dawn.

She stood atop the Scarred Walls, her priestess robes whipping in the ash-laden wind, and watched General Varak's war machines feed on her city's orchards. The air tasted of char. Below her, the last of the defenders scrambled to brace positions that would not hold, their movements desperate, like men reaching for a rope that wasn't there. Six weeks of siege had taught her to read a soldier's body from this height. Every line of them said the same thing. They knew, too.

"High Priestess." Captain Kaelen's voice cut through the wind. He climbed the final steps to her position, his armor dented from the afternoon's bombardment, blood that was not his own streaked through his graying beard. "The war council waits."

She didn't turn. In the distance another volley of flaming pitch arced through the dark, bright and slow, almost gentle. "Tell me something I don't already know, Kaelen."

"The outer walls go within hours. The northern gate sooner." He came to stand beside her, close enough that she could smell the battle on him, sweat and steel and that particular stench of fear no courage ever quite masked.

She looked at him then, this man who had served her father and now served her with the same unwavering loyalty. They both knew what he was not saying, that it did not matter whether they held until dawn. Varak had three soldiers for every one of theirs, and his engines turned ancient stone to dust as a miller turned grain.
