The Story

Every door has a toll

When a freak surge tears through a college presentation, Lina Rivers learns the static she has felt her whole life was never anxiety. It is a language. Bridges are thin places, and Millbrook's Riverside Bridge sings to her bones.

Kaelan, an exiled guardian from the twilight realm of Aethora, names her what the Majikai Court fears most: a Bridge-Walker, a living gateway between worlds. Every crossing opens a door. Every door demands a price, and the price is paid in memories.

As impossible anomalies ripple outward, Lina's found family becomes her only lifeline: Maya, a pre-med genius who builds tech to read the unseeable, and Marcus, a synesthetic artist who maps the world in color. Together they uncover a hidden war between a glittering, dangerous Majikai Council and Mordecai, an ArchMajikai zealot willing to retune the world's bridges and crack open a ravenous elsewhere.

From neon-lit repair shops to bioluminescent gardens and a mask-laced mirror hall, Lina is pulled into politics, bargains, and betrayals. With reality fraying and a hungry Void pressing close, she must master a power that steals her past even as it might save her future.

Bridges connect. Bridges consume.
If the only way to save two worlds is to sacrifice pieces of yourself, how much of your past would you give to secure the future?

The Triad

The Bridge

The Scholar

What It's About

Memory, trust, and the cost of power

Memory as identity

Every crossing erases a piece of Lina's past. What is left of you when the price of saving everyone is forgetting them?

Control vs. trust

The villain offers control. The Triad offers trust. Every real victory means letting go.

Being made a weapon

Every faction has a name for Lina: asset, hazard, key. Only the people who love her still call her Lina.

Found family as resonance

Lina's power is unstable alone. With Maya and Marcus, the cold finally goes quiet. The bond is not sentiment. It is the mechanism.
